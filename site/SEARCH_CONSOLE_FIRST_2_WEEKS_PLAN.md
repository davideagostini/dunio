# Search Console: First 2 Weeks Plan

## Goal

Use the first 14 days after adding `dunio.app` to Google Search Console to:

- confirm indexing is working
- make sure new SEO pages are discoverable
- catch technical issues early
- identify the first useful search queries
- decide whether content or internal linking needs adjustment

This plan is intentionally simple and practical.

---

## Priority Pages

These are the pages worth checking first:

- `https://dunio.app/`
- `https://dunio.app/resources`
- `https://dunio.app/shared-finance-app-for-couples`
- `https://dunio.app/couples-budget-app`
- `https://dunio.app/household-budget-app`
- `https://dunio.app/household-expense-tracker`
- `https://dunio.app/net-worth-tracker-for-couples`

---

## Day 1

### 1. Confirm the sitemap was submitted

In Search Console:

- open `Sitemaps`
- confirm `https://dunio.app/sitemap.xml` is listed
- check that Google accepted it successfully

What you want to see:

- sitemap status is successful
- discovered URLs count is not zero

### 2. Inspect the most important pages

Use `URL Inspection` on:

- homepage
- `Resources`
- `couples-budget-app`
- `household-budget-app`
- `shared-finance-app-for-couples`

If available, request indexing for pages that are not yet indexed.

### 3. Check indexing coverage quickly

Open `Page indexing` and look for obvious warnings such as:

- crawled but not indexed
- discovered but not indexed
- blocked by `robots.txt`
- duplicate without user-selected canonical

At this stage, do not panic if pages are not indexed yet. Day 1 is mostly baseline.

---

## Day 3

### 1. Re-check the priority pages

Inspect again:

- homepage
- `Resources`
- the two budget pages

Questions to answer:

- has Google crawled them?
- are they indexed yet?
- is the canonical recognized correctly?

### 2. Check if the homepage is the strongest hub

Review whether the homepage, footer, and `Resources` page are linking cleanly to:

- `couples-budget-app`
- `household-budget-app`
- `shared-finance-app-for-couples`
- `net-worth-tracker-for-couples`

If a page is still not indexed after being requested, internal linking becomes more important.

### 3. Watch for inconsistent messaging

Do a quick check on public copy:

- beta wording is consistent
- no outdated “download now” language remains
- pages do not overpromise store availability

This matters because Google may index stale copy quickly.

---

## Day 5

### 1. Check Page Indexing report again

Look for patterns, not just single URLs.

If multiple pages are stuck in:

- `Discovered - currently not indexed`
- `Crawled - currently not indexed`

then the likely causes are:

- Google still needs time
- pages are too similar
- pages need stronger internal linking
- pages are not yet seen as distinct enough

### 2. Compare the two new budget pages

Manually review:

- `couples-budget-app`
- `household-budget-app`

Ask:

- are they clearly distinct?
- do they target different search intents?
- does each page justify its own existence?

If they feel too similar, expand one angle rather than leaving them as near-duplicates.

---

## Day 7

### 1. Check Performance for first impressions

Open `Performance` and look at:

- total impressions
- first queries
- pages with impressions

Do not expect much traffic yet. At this stage even a small number of impressions is useful.

### 2. Look for early query signals

You are looking for queries like:

- `shared expense tracker for couples`
- `couples budget app`
- `household budget app`
- `net worth tracker for couples`
- branded queries like `dunio app`

If you see impressions for related but unexpected terms, note them. Those can become future content opportunities.

### 3. Decide whether metadata needs adjustment

If impressions are appearing but CTR is weak, the next levers are:

- page title
- meta description
- screenshot/visual relevance in social shares

Do not change everything too early. Only note candidates for iteration.

---

## Day 10

### 1. Review what is indexed vs. what is not

Make a small table for:

- page URL
- indexed: yes/no
- requested indexing: yes/no
- impressions: yes/no

This helps avoid guessing.

### 2. Strengthen pages that are weak

If one page has no indexing progress or no impressions while similar pages do:

- improve internal links to it
- make the intro more distinct
- sharpen the target keyword in the headline and first paragraph

Likely candidates for this kind of review:

- `couples-budget-app`
- `household-budget-app`

### 3. Check if the beta CTA is working conceptually

This is not a Search Console metric directly, but it matters:

- is the site consistently positioned around beta access?
- do SEO pages push toward the beta clearly enough?

If not, conversion may be weak even when traffic starts to arrive.

---

## Day 14

### 1. Do a two-week review

At the end of two weeks, answer these:

- how many of the priority pages are indexed?
- which pages received impressions?
- which queries appeared first?
- which pages have zero visibility?

### 2. Decide the next move

If indexing is healthy and impressions are starting:

- keep the structure
- publish 1–2 more targeted pages only if supported by query demand

If indexing is weak:

- improve page differentiation
- strengthen internal linking
- request indexing again only for the most important pages

If impressions are present but queries are off-target:

- revise titles and intros
- clarify page intent
- tighten keyword focus

---

## Recommended Weekly Routine

### Week 1

- check Search Console on Day 1, 3, 5, 7
- focus on indexing and crawl status
- avoid over-editing too early

### Week 2

- check Search Console on Day 10 and 14
- focus on impressions, early queries, and page differentiation
- make only targeted changes

---

## What Not To Do

- do not submit every page repeatedly every day
- do not rewrite all titles after 48 hours
- do not assume “not indexed yet” means something is broken
- do not add lots of weak content before the first pages settle

---

## Success Signals

By the end of two weeks, good signs would be:

- homepage indexed
- `Resources` indexed
- at least some of the new SEO pages indexed
- first impressions in `Performance`
- first branded or category-adjacent queries appearing

That is enough to know the setup is working.

---

## Next Possible Actions After Week 2

- refine titles and descriptions based on query data
- improve internal linking if some pages lag
- add one new page only if a clear keyword opportunity appears
- align Play Store copy and website copy even more closely if needed
