## 2. Product Lesson

### LinkedIn

```text
One thing I keep learning while building Dunio:

A finance app does not become more useful just because it shows more data.

In many cases, it becomes less usable.

The hard part is deciding what to leave out so the important numbers stay obvious:
net worth, spending, cash flow, assets, liabilities, and recurring items.

Simple is harder than it looks.
```

### X


## 3. Problem-First

### LinkedIn

```text
A lot of shared finance pain is not about math.

It is about coordination.

Who added the transaction?
Are we looking at the same numbers?
Did we both update the same month?
Where do recurring bills live?
Are assets and liabilities tracked in one place?

Dunio is built around that coordination problem, not just around expense logging.
```

### X

```text

That problem matters a lot.
```

## 4. Feature Deep Dive: Widget

### LinkedIn

```text
Widgets are a small feature, but they say a lot about the product philosophy behind Dunio.

If checking spending requires too much friction, most people simply stop checking.

That is why I like features that reduce the distance between "I should update this" and "done".

The closer finance tracking gets to everyday behavior, the more useful it becomes.
```

### X

```text
Widgets are a small feature, but they reflect a bigger product principle:
```
