Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 26, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Summ collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use the Summ Android app.

Summ is a mobile-first app for shared household finance tracking. It uses Google Sign-In, Firebase Authentication, and Cloud Firestore.

This Privacy Policy reflects the app's current behavior. Review it with legal counsel if needed before relying on it in production.

Operator

  • Legal name: Davide Simone Agostini

1. Information we collect

Account information

When you sign in with Google, Summ may collect and store:

  • your Google account email address
  • your name
  • your profile photo URL
  • your user ID

This information is used to authenticate you, restore your session, and associate you with a household.

Household and finance data

When you use the app, Summ stores the information you or other household members enter, including:

  • household name and household settings
  • household currency
  • member role information
  • invites sent to join a household
  • categories
  • transactions, including date, description, amount, type, and category
  • assets and liabilities, including monthly snapshots and history
  • recurring transactions
  • month close status

Because Summ supports shared households, information entered in a household may be visible to other members of the same household.

Technical and usage data

Summ uses Firebase services to operate. Firebase and Google may process technical data needed to provide authentication, database access, security, and infrastructure reliability, such as IP address, device and app identifiers, request metadata, and service logs.

Summ does not currently include advertising SDKs in the Android app.

Summ does not currently include analytics SDKs in the Android app.

2. How we use your information

  • authenticate users with Google Sign-In
  • create and manage user accounts and households
  • store and display shared household finance data
  • enable invite and membership flows
  • show widgets and Quick Settings tile content related to your signed-in household
  • keep the app secure, reliable, and functional
  • comply with legal obligations where required

3. How information is shared

We do not sell your personal information.

We may share information in the following limited ways:

With other household members

Summ is a shared-household app. Data stored in a household is intended to be visible to members of that household according to the app's access model.

With service providers

Summ relies on service providers such as Google Firebase to provide authentication, hosting, and database functionality.

For legal or safety reasons

We may disclose information if required by law or if reasonably necessary to protect rights, safety, security, or the integrity of the service.

4. Legal bases

If you are in the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, or a similar jurisdiction, we may process your information under one or more of the following legal bases:

  • performance of a contract, to provide the app to you
  • legitimate interests, such as keeping the service secure and improving reliability
  • compliance with legal obligations
  • consent, where consent is required

5. Data retention

We keep account and household data for as long as it is needed to operate the service, maintain shared household functionality, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements.

At this time, Summ does not provide a fully self-service in-app account deletion flow. If you want your account or related data deleted, use the account deletion page at /account-deletion/ or contact us at dsagostini@gmail.com.

If you are a member of a shared household, deleting data may affect other household members.

6. Security

We use Firebase Authentication, Firestore security rules, and reasonable technical measures intended to protect data. However, no method of storage or transmission is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. International transfers

Your information may be processed in countries other than your own, including by service providers such as Google. Where required, we rely on appropriate safeguards for international data transfers.

8. Your rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights such as:

  • access to your personal data
  • correction of inaccurate data
  • deletion of your data
  • restriction of processing
  • objection to certain processing
  • data portability

To make a request, contact dsagostini@gmail.com.

9. Children's privacy

Summ is not directed to children under 13, and where applicable not directed to children below the minimum digital consent age in the relevant jurisdiction. If you believe a child has provided personal data, contact dsagostini@gmail.com.

Summ relies on third-party services including:
