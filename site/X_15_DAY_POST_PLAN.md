# Dunio X 15-Day Plan

Two posts per day for 15 days. All posts are in English and kept under 280 characters.

## Day 1

1. Building Dunio: a mobile-first Android app for shared household finance. Track net worth, expenses, assets, liabilities, and recurring payments in one calm workspace for couples and families.

2. Most finance apps still feel single-player. Dunio is built around a shared household, so two people can track the same finances, dashboard, assets, and transactions together.

## Day 2

3. Dunio helps couples track money together without spreadsheet chaos. Shared expenses, net worth, assets, and recurring payments stay in one place.

4. Shared finance should be simple: one household, one dashboard, clear numbers, low friction. That’s the core idea behind Dunio.

## Day 3

5. Dunio is built for people who want clarity, not accounting-software complexity. Net worth, expenses, assets, liabilities, and recurring payments in one Android app.

6. A good finance app should be easy to maintain daily. Dunio focuses on fast entry, clear views, and a shared household model that actually fits real life.

## Day 4

7. Dunio works as a shared expense tracker for couples, but it also gives you the bigger picture: net worth, assets, liabilities, cash flow, and recurring items.

8. Tracking shared finances is easier when both people see the same numbers. Dunio keeps the dashboard, transactions, and assets aligned inside one household.

## Day 5

9. Dunio includes recurring payments, so repeat bills and repeated income don’t have to be entered from scratch every month.

10. Shared finance is not just spending. Dunio helps track net worth too, so couples can see where they stand, not only what they spent.

## Day 6

11. Dunio supports assets and liabilities in the same shared workspace. That makes monthly net worth tracking much easier to keep current.

12. Quick entry matters. The less friction there is, the more likely people are to keep their shared finances updated every day.

## Day 7

13. Dunio includes Android widgets for quick access to spending data right from the home screen. Less friction, better habits.

14. I also built a Quick Settings Tile for Dunio, so adding a transaction can be faster than opening the full app flow.

## Day 8

15. Dunio is free to use and open source. I want shared finance tracking to be accessible, transparent, and practical.

16. Open source matters here. If you use Dunio, you can see how it works, follow development, and trust that the product stays focused.

## Day 9

17. Dunio is built with Kotlin, Jetpack Compose, Firebase Auth, and Firestore. Android-first, mobile-first, and designed around shared household finance.

18. I’m intentionally keeping Dunio lightweight. The goal is not to build accounting software. The goal is to make shared finance tracking actually usable.

## Day 10

19. Many couples end up sharing one login, duplicating entries, or syncing spreadsheets manually. Dunio is built to avoid that with a real shared household model.

20. Dunio lets household members share the same dashboard, finance data, and monthly view, with roles like owner and member.

## Day 11

21. If you manage money with a partner, what is the hardest part today: shared expenses, recurring bills, net worth, or just keeping everything updated?

22. I’m building Dunio around one idea: the best finance app is the one both people will actually keep using.

## Day 12

23. Dunio also supports month-by-month tracking, so the household can review progress over time instead of staring at disconnected numbers.

24. The app includes savings rate, cash flow, and financial runway views too. I want the key numbers to be visible without overwhelming the user.

## Day 13

25. If Dunio sounds useful to you, I’m looking for people who want to try it early and share feedback before wider release. Reply here or DM me.

26. Early testers are especially helpful if you already track money with a partner or household and know where current tools fall short.

## Day 14

27. Dunio is for couples, families, and shared households who want one calm place for finances: expenses, assets, liabilities, recurring payments, and net worth.

28. If you like the direction of Dunio and want to support development, donations help keep the project free, open source, and improving over time.

## Day 15

29. Dunio is a free, open-source Android app for shared household finance. Built for real daily use, not just for setup screenshots.

30. Building in public means improving in public too. Dunio is still evolving, but the goal stays the same: shared finance that feels simple, clear, and calm.

## Extra Posts: Month Close

31. Dunio includes a month close flow, so each month can be reviewed and locked cleanly instead of turning into a moving target forever.

32. Closing the month helps keep history clean. Dunio makes it easier to treat each month as a finished snapshot, not a messy draft that keeps changing.

33. Shared finance gets clearer when months have boundaries. Dunio includes month close so the household can review, confirm, and move forward with confidence.

34. I added month close to Dunio because finance tracking gets harder when old months stay open forever. Clean periods make reviews much easier.

## Extra Posts: Data Export

35. Dunio also supports data export, so household finance data can be saved outside the app when needed. Simple tools should still respect portability.

36. Export matters. Dunio lets households export data as CSV or JSON, which makes backups and external analysis much easier.

37. I want Dunio to stay lightweight, but that doesn’t mean locking data in. Export is built in so people can keep control of their own household records.

38. Shared finance tools should not trap your data. Dunio includes export options so your household can keep a backup or work with data elsewhere if needed.
